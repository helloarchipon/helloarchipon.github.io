La Petite Maison - Small in Size, Big on Utility

02 Jul, 2020 · Archipon

Check out this sexy little number. This tiny maison has been designed by the French architecture firm arba.

Sometimes wood can be too much of a good thing, but I think it strikes a nice balance here (although I personally would have stopped at the walls). Plus you need to factor in that it's meant to function as a little escape for the owners and their family, a little wooden cabin in the woods.

The project is about building a minimal house to make the most of the beautiful existing garden and the proximity of the sea; like a shelter to inhabit the site in a comfortable and dynamic way. - arba

As always, when going small the outdoors plays a larger part in the success of the space. They've got big-ass windows downstairs, and a much more enclosed and private upstairs (the loft).

To bring a large pleasure in this small area, the challenge is to make the people feel inhabiting the garden.

This is the kind of getaway I dream about owning myself. Something small, remote, and hidden. La Petite Maison is a place I could see myself totally kicking back and relaxing in.
